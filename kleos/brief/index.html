The Vision

"Mothership of different spaceships. Each spaceship holds different keys."

— Einat

This is what you told me Kleos is building toward. Not a single event series. A platform. Multiple practitioners, multiple modalities — erostatic dance, shibari, tantra, somatic work, erotic meditation, femdom — all delivered under one brand, held together by one community. The experiences are the spaceships. The community is the mothership.

Everything in this document — the improvements, the decisions — should be read through this lens. We are not just professionalizing a monthly dance event. We are building the infrastructure for something that can grow beyond the two of you.

Section II

What Is Working

These are the things we protect, not change.

  • The container quality. 95% attendance rate, ~50% return rate. The space you hold IS the product.
  • Community trust. 115 past attendees in an exclusive group. This is rare.
  • Word of mouth. Your primary acquisition channel. Harder to build than any campaign.
  • Positioning instinct. You already know what you are not. That clarity protects the brand.

Section III

What We Can Improve

You Do Not Know What You Are Earning

Neither of you tracks average revenue per event or per person. You know the range but not where people land. Making decisions without this data means flying blind.

The Registration Flow Is a Bottleneck You Built for Yourselves

The gender balance check is right and should stay. But today every registration triggers a manual decision plus a manual deposit request. 30 registrations = 30 individual actions. This is solvable.

People Do Not Know What They Are Signing Up For

Einat said it herself: missing explanations, many people have fear. Fear is normal for this category. The question is whether you help people through it or leave them alone with it.

No One Owns Anything

Einat, Nani, Rodrigo, Leo, Kimi — who decides when a new event launches? Who sends communications? Who manages the WhatsApp group? When everyone is responsible, things fall through.

Section IV

What We Need to Decide

These questions only you can answer. Bring your answers to the session.

1

What is the Kleos DNA — the thread that runs through every experience?

Erostatic dance and a femdom workshop are very different rooms. What is the value, the aesthetic, the feeling that makes both unmistakably Kleos? Without this, the collective becomes incoherent as it grows. This is the question that shapes everything else — the brand, who you let in, how you onboard practitioners.

2

What does it take to deliver under the Kleos brand?

If Kleos puts its name on someone's work, what needs to be true about them first? Standards, values alignment, quality of container — and in return, what does Kleos give them that they could not build alone? The answer to this question is your practitioner model. Without it, growth means brand dilution.

3

What holds the community together between events?

115 people in a WhatsApp group is a foundation. But a platform community needs a reason to stay engaged beyond event announcements. A shared identity. A recurring ritual. A sense of belonging to something. What is that for Kleos — and who is responsible for tending it?

4

How do you make decisions as the collective grows?

Today, two founders decide everything. When there are five practitioners delivering under Kleos, who decides on new offerings, brand standards, who gets to join? You need a governance model before you need it — not after a conflict forces the question.

🏗 Formation
Who owns what — and what happens if a co-founder leaves?
Without an architect

Generic template from the internet. Handshake deal with your co-founder. Incorporated wherever a friend suggested.

With an architect

Ownership designed for the next 3 rounds. Entity in the right jurisdiction for your capital strategy. IP locked to the company.

Founders' agreement Entity structure memo IP assignments
💰 Capital
What does your cap table look like after this round — and did you just lose control?
Without an architect

Lawyer sends a SAFE they downloaded. Cap table in a spreadsheet nobody trusts. Investors get whatever they ask for.

With an architect

Instrument structured for your scenario. Cap table modeled through Series B. Control provisions that protect the founder.

Cap table model Investment docs Control provisions analysis
📈 Growth
How does your board actually make decisions — and are you ready for the next round?
Without an architect

Board meets "when we need to." Key hire gets 2% because that's what they asked for. Round prep starts 2 weeks before the raise.

With an architect

Board charter with decision rights. ESOP designed against a dilution model. Structural audit 6 months before the round.

Governance framework ESOP design Pre-round structural audit

Without an architect

New market = new entity = new mess. Acquisition takes 9 months of scrambling. Exit readiness is a concept, not a document.

With an architect

Jurisdiction playbook before you land. Deal architecture in weeks. Exit-ready structure maintained continuously.

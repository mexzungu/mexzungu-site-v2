For Nick Hu and Lukas Lukoschek, Kabisa

You are building the spine of East Africa's electric future. Let's protect the terms.

Prepared specifically for Kabisa. Valid for 30 days from May 2, 2026.

01 / What we heard
You have the traction. The risk is in the terms.

Kabisa holds roughly 80% of public EV charging in Rwanda. In one quarter, volumes jumped from 107,000 kWh to 832,000 kWh. That is not a projection. That is a machine that works.

Now comes the part that breaks companies. An African VC just put a $1.5M SAFE on the table with a $12M cap, a board seat before conversion, and liquidation terms that would sit senior to every SAFE your early backers hold. The cap is too low for what you have built. The governance language is written for a company that does not have leverage. You do.

At the same time, Mount Meru Group wants in on the Rwanda network and the Dar es Salaam–Kigali corridor. They are a $900M conglomerate and they will not give you equity in return. Which means the structure of this partnership determines how much of it you actually control and whether the financial history flows through your books or theirs.

Two workstreams. Two live deals. Both need someone in the room who has seen what these terms cost founders when they go wrong.

02 / Scope
Two active mandates. One aligned advisor.

Workstream 1 (corporate structure) is parked until the bigger round is in play. The two live mandates are the equity round and the Mount Meru SPV. These are activated from the date of the engagement letter.

Workstream 2 — Active
Fundraising Strategy + Capital Raise
ACV negotiation + future equity and debt rounds
  • Fundraising strategy and investor memorandum. The document that tells your story in investor language, built around the numbers you already have.
  • ACV term sheet redline within 48 hours of receipt — annotated positions on every clause: cap, board seat, reserved matters, liquidation priority, MFN structure. Same turnaround for any subsequent sheet.
  • Plain-language negotiation memo. What to hold, what to give, where your walkaway is, and why. No jargon. Decision-ready.
  • Pepe on every investor call until the money clears. The joint ACV call, every term sheet negotiation call, every closing call. 48-hour availability for anything else that comes up.
  • Future rounds. This workstream covers any equity or debt raise in Kabisa HoldCo within the engagement period, not just ACV.
Workstream 3 — Active
SPV Formation + Capital Structure
Mount Meru Group partnership — first activated mandate
  • SPV structure for the Mount Meru partnership. Jurisdiction, constitutional documents, governance. Built so that revenue flows through Kabisa's books and builds your financial history for future capital raises.
  • Capital structure across instruments. Equity, debt, structured finance — investor-agnostic so you are not locked into one type of partner.
  • Clean exit mechanism. A buyout option structured so Mount Meru can eventually take over on agreed terms, without the handover becoming a legal fight.
  • Billing and customer ownership architecture. Framed as a technology decision, not a control decision. Makes it harder to negotiate away and easier to defend operationally.
  • Security package. Built around Kabisa's assets: the charging infrastructure, the revenue streams, or both — depending on what the capital partner requires.
On future Workstream 3 mandates. Each additional SPV or infrastructure transaction within this workstream is activated by a brief written exchange confirming the mandate name and scope. No success fee applies to a transaction that has not been activated. The Mount Meru Group charging network partnership is active from the date of the engagement letter.

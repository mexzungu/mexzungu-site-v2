Here is what we can build together.

Prepared specifically for HavenPay. Valid for 30 days from May 1, 2026.

01 / What we heard
You are building two things at once.

HavenPay Foundation receives grant capital from DFIs, foundations, and UNHCR. It funds evaluation, policy engagement, community ambassador infrastructure, and the Refugee Financial Identity Stack blueprint. This entity makes the model credible to institutions.

HavenPay Technologies owns the platform and IP, holds the Choice Bank BaaS relationship, issues equity to investors, and generates all five revenue streams. This entity makes the model attractive to capital.

You have the architecture in your head. The problem is that the architecture in your head is not the same as a legally defensible, investor-ready structure. The dual-entity model only works if the IP licensing terms between Foundation and Technologies are watertight, grant compliance requirements are structurally separated from equity upside, and the cap table in Technologies is clean before the first institutional cheque lands.

That is not a legal problem. It is a Venture Architecture problem. And it is exactly what we do.

02 / What we will build
Two workstreams. Activate one or both.

Each deliverable in Workstream 1 is a decision-ready document — not a deck, not a framework. Something you can hand to a lawyer, an investor, or a regulator and have it hold up.

Workstream 1
Venture Architecture

The full architecture blueprint. Entity structure, IP, capital stack, and investor narrative — all four deliverables, built before you raise, so you are not improvising under term sheet pressure.

Entity Architecture Memo
Foundation and Technologies HoldCo structure, jurisdiction recommendation, board composition, decision rights, and which entity owns the Choice Bank BaaS relationship from day one.
IP Licensing Framework
Full IP asset inventory: HavenScore, the Refugee Financial Identity Stack, transaction data protocols, platform technology, brand. Licensing terms defensible to a DFI grant officer and a Series A investor at the same time. Carve-outs for open-access grant deliverables.
Fundraising Architecture
How the $250K grant, $500K pre-seed, and DFI debt facility work together without destroying each other. Instrument recommendation, cap table design, and the evidence base Phase 2 needs to unlock.
Investor Narrative Brief
One page. Three audiences: the Systems Disruptor family office, the DFI grant officer, the future Series A. Each hears the same story through their own lens.
Timeline 4 weeks from kickoff
